Equipment Information


Nearly all mobility equipment has a weight capacity for your safety. Do not exceed it!



Wheelchairs

Electric or power /manual /recliner your specialized chair may have different features/ adjustments/ accessories for your personalized comfort or need. Here are just a few. Joystick controlled motion to go in all directions. A neutral lever(s) is used (to push power wheelchairs manually instead of battery power.)

Adjustable foot rests, and arm pads, special head supports,removable arms and, wheel locks, hand rims, seat restraints and lap trays. The front tires are known as “castors." Some of the older manual wheelchairs will “unfold” with ease if one side of the chair is slightly tilted while unfolding it.


Power (electric) wheelchairs

Power (electric) wheelchairs can weigh from about a hundred to several hundred pounds, controlled by a joystick and/ or mouthpeice for motion and other customized functions. Equipped with battery charger or charging port.


Manual wheelchairs

Manual wheelchairs are self propelled by user’s arms or by an assistant. Some wheelchairs are designed to support weights up to and exeeding 500lbs. Many of these are known as "heavy duty" wheelchairs.


Recliner wheelchairs

Recliner wheelchairs are self propelled by user’s arms or by an assistant. This wheelchair has the ability to recline its user at nearly 180 degrees for a specific need. This particular function requires an assistant. The tilt mechanism located under hand grips are sometimes mistaken for hand brakes, thus causing potential damage to the user if the assistant is not trained. It has additional accessories also for the head and foot.


Scooters

Scooters Adjustable tiller /swivel seat as well as removable in most cases. It has arm rests that retract or extend. This also has a controlled speed dial and gears to go forward or reverse. Most scooters have three wheels. Many users of this type of transportation are not aware that your speed controler can be set at “fast” as well as “slow”pace. Portable (can be broken down for easier transport via automobile) has a neutral lever (to push manually instead of battery power-like that of the power wheelchair) Equipped with battery charger or charging port.


Transport chairs

Transport chairs not to be confused with wheelchairs –are not self propelled like a wheelchair. At first glance, it may look like a wheelchair. It usually has all four wheels that are approximately all the same size. They have wheel locks and additional accessories. They are much lighter than a wheelchair and can be broken down and, or folded for easy transport.


Knee walkers

Knee walkers have adjustable seat height and handlebars. This usually has three to four wheels. Knee walkers are guided via two handlebars. It is rode as though you see young children riding toy scooters, except you would rest your knee on an elongated padded seat, instead of having both feet near the wheels. It is then propelled with your one”good” leg and guided with both hands to smoothly get you to your destination. This item is sometimes used to substitute the use of crutches.
